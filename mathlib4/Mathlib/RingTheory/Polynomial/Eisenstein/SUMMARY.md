# Eisenstein

## Overview

The `Eisenstein/` directory contains the theory of Eisenstein polynomials and the classical Eisenstein irreducibility criterion. A polynomial `f : R[X]` is Eisenstein at an ideal `ğ“Ÿ` if its leading coefficient is not in `ğ“Ÿ`, all non-leading coefficients are in `ğ“Ÿ`, and the constant term is not in `ğ“Ÿ^2`. The main result proves that primitive Eisenstein polynomials at prime ideals are irreducible. The directory also defines weakly Eisenstein and distinguished polynomials, provides a generalized Eisenstein criterion, and includes integrality results for algebraic extensions generated by Eisenstein polynomials.
