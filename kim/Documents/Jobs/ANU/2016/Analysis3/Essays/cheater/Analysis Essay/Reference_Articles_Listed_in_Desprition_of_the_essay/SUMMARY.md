## Overview

Nine academic research articles on fractional Brownian motion (fBm) theory, construction methods, stochastic calculus, and applications, all in PDF format ranging from 146KB to 529KB (22-29 pages each). The collection includes the foundational 1968 Mandelbrot & Van Ness paper introducing fractional Brownian motions as a family of Gaussian processes with the Hurst parameter H, plus contemporary (2006-era) papers on stochastic calculus techniques including Malliavin calculus, rough path construction using Volterra representations, PDE connections, time fractional processes, and fractal dimension analysis for continuous time random walks.
