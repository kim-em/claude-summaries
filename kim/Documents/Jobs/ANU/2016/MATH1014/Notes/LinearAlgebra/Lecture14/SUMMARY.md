# Lecture14

## Overview

Lecture 14 focuses on matrix diagonalization, addressing the question "When is a matrix A similar to a diagonal matrix?" The lecture reviews eigenvectors and eigenvalues, then introduces the diagonalization theorem and the factorization A = PDP^(-1). Key topics include the advantages of diagonal matrices (easy computation of determinants, eigenvalues, and matrix powers), the definition of diagonalizable matrices, and the relationship between the columns of P (eigenvectors) and the diagonal entries of D (eigenvalues). The lecture demonstrates how to compute high powers of matrices efficiently using diagonalization.
