# Lecture17

## Overview

Lecture 17 materials on complex eigenvalues and eigenvectors for MATH1014 Linear Algebra course (ANU, Semester 2 2015). The lecture explores what happens when real matrices have complex eigenvalues, introducing complex eigenvectors and proving that real 2×2 matrices with complex eigenvalues can be represented as rotation-scaling transformations (A = PCP⁻¹ decomposition). Contains comprehensive LaTeX source for beamer slides (705 lines), a supplementary 329-line complex numbers addendum covering field properties and arithmetic operations, and a rich collection of 8 supporting diagrams. The diagrams provide extensive geometric visualization: transformation actions showing rotation and scaling behavior, trajectory plots demonstrating iteration behavior (including spiral/elliptical patterns), angle diagrams for polar/complex number representation, matrix power visualizations, and a detailed complex numbers reference slide from an earlier course (MATH1013 2008). The visual materials strongly emphasize the geometric interpretation of complex eigenvalues as combined rotation (angle φ) and scaling (radius r) operations in ℝ².
